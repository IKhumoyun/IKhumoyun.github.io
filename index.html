Why it is time
to act now?

Because if we don’t act now, tomorrow it’s consequences will reach all of us

For centuries, the Aral Sea, world’s fourth largest inland lake, was offering its treasures and sustained many settlements, where people enjoyed prosperous lives as fishermen, farmers, herders, craftsmen and traders.

In just past 60-80 years, however, it has lost 90% of its size and transformed into the Aralkum desert by making catastrophic changes in biodiversity - many types of fish, animals and plants completely despaired from the region and influencing on people’s health.

For the last several years diseases like cancer and TB dramatically increased among population, as well as cases of newborns with difficulties in development. Toxic salts and sand are being picked up by winds and carried out to hundred and thousands of kilometers.

Studies show that each year, winds from Aral Sea carry 100 million tons of toxic sand and salts that reach out European cities, glaciers of Island, forests in Norway and even Antarctica.

Saxaul can help stopping the spread of toxic salts and desertification - one tree can fix and hold up to 4 tons of sand.

  • The area of Aral Sea is slightly less than the size of Ireland
  • The sea lost 60,000 km2 of its size (double size of the territory of Belgium)
  • 50% of the flora and fauna is lost, including: 11 types of fish, 11 species plants, 26 species of birds, 12 species of mammals
  • Frequent droughts and dust storms – 75 milln tonnes of toxic salts and sand are spread every year
  • Cancer, tuberculosis (TB) cases are 50% more higher than in other regions
  • Only 50% of population have access to drinking water
  • Poverty rate is the highest in the region.
